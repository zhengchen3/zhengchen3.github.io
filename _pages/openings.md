# Positions

We welcome motivated students and research assistants who are excited about reliable AI for health, EEG representation learning, and multi-omics data analysis.

All applicants should first read our [Research page]({{ site.url }}{{ site.baseurl }}/research/) to understand our current projects and see whether your interests align.

## PhD positions

We typically have one PhD student opening per year. Suitable backgrounds include computer science, biomedical engineering, and bioinformatics or computational biology. Current PhD projects focus on EEG representation learning and brain modeling, agentic AI for biomedical data analysis, and multi-omics integration with knowledge graphs.

If you are interested in a PhD position, please send an email to [chenz@sanken.osaka-u.ac.jp](mailto:chenz@sanken.osaka-u.ac.jp) with:

1. A recent CV (education, publications, relevant projects).  
2. A short paragraph (5–10 sentences) explaining which of our research directions interests you most and how your past experience prepares you for this work.  

Please use the subject line: `Application – PhD`.

## Research assistant (RA) positions

We also recruit research assistants on projects at the interface of AI, software engineering, and computational biology. Below are two typical RA profiles.

### RA – Agentic AI for drug discovery

You will help build agentic AI data analysis pipelines for drug discovery projects using real-world data.

- Comfortable with Python and basic TypeScript/JavaScript.  
- Some experience with LLM agents, deployment, and tool calling.  
- Familiarity with LangChain, LangGraph, or similar frameworks for agent orchestration (HITL, durable execution).  
- Basic prompt engineering and database knowledge (SQL/NoSQL) are a plus.  

### RA – Bioinformatics / multi-omics

You will work on genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, and metabolomics data analysis and integration.

- Experience with R and/or Python, and bioinformatics tools such as Bioconductor.  
- Familiarity with experimental design and data generation workflows in genomics, transcriptomics, or metabolomics.  
- Background in computational or systems biology (e.g., stoichiometric or kinetic metabolic models) or multi-omics disease/metabolic data analysis is highly welcome.  

To apply for an RA position, please email [chenz@sanken.osaka-u.ac.jp](mailto:chenz@sanken.osaka-u.ac.jp) with:

1. A CV highlighting technical skills and relevant projects.  
2. A short statement (5–10 sentences) indicating whether you are more interested in the agentic AI track or the bioinformatics/multi-omics track, and briefly describing one project that best demonstrates your fit.  

Please use one of the subject lines: `Application – RA (Agentic AI)` or `Application – RA (Bioinformatics)`.
