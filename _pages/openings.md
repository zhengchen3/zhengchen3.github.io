# Positions

We welcome motivated students and research assistants who are excited about reliable AI for health, EEG representation learning, and multi-omics data analysis.

All applicants should first read our [Research page]({{ site.url }}{{ site.baseurl }}/research/) to understand our current projects and see whether your interests align.

## PhD positions

We typically have **one PhD student opening per year**.

- **Backgrounds**: computer science, biomedical engineering, bioinformatics/computational biology, or related fields.  
- **Topics**: EEG representation learning and brain modeling, agentic AI for biomedical data analysis, multi-omics integration and knowledge graphs.  

If you are interested in a PhD position, please send an email to [chenz@sanken.osaka-u.ac.jp](mailto:chenz@sanken.osaka-u.ac.jp) with:

- An up-to-date **CV** (including education, publications, and relevant projects).  
- A short **paragraph (5–10 sentences)** explaining (i) which of our research directions interests you most and (ii) how your past experience prepares you for this work.  

Please use the subject line **“Application – PhD”**.

## Research assistant (RA) positions

We also recruit research assistants on projects at the interface of AI, software engineering, and computational biology. Below are two typical RA profiles.

### RA – Agentic AI for Drug Discovery

You will help build **agentic AI data analysis pipelines** for drug discovery projects using real-world data.

- Comfortable with **Python** and basic **TypeScript/JavaScript**.  
- Familiar with **LLM agents**, deployment, and tool calling.  
- Experience with **LangChain / LangGraph** (or similar frameworks) for agent orchestration (HITL, durable execution).  
- Good **prompt engineering** skills and basic understanding of databases (SQL/NoSQL) is a plus.  

### RA – Bioinformatics / Multi-omics

You will work on **genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, and metabolomics** data analysis and integration.

- Experience with **R** and/or **Python**, and bioinformatics ecosystems such as **Bioconductor**.  
- Familiar with experimental design and data generation workflows in genomics, transcriptomics, or metabolomics.  
- Background in **computational biology/systems biology** (e.g., stoichiometric or kinetic metabolic models) or **multi-omics disease/metabolic data analysis** is highly welcome.  

To apply for an RA position, please email [chenz@sanken.osaka-u.ac.jp](mailto:chenz@sanken.osaka-u.ac.jp) with:

- A **CV** highlighting technical skills and relevant projects.  
- A brief **statement (5–10 sentences)** indicating whether you are more interested in the *Agentic AI* or *Bioinformatics/Multi-omics* track, and describing one project that best demonstrates your fit.  

Please use the subject line **“Application – RA (Agentic AI)”** or **“Application – RA (Bioinformatics)”**.
